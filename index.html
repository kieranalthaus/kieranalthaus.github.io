About

I am a Ph.D candidate in the Committee on Evolutionary Biology at the University of Chicago, advised by Dr. Andrew Hipp. My research spans population genetics, phylogenetics, biogeography, and speciation, with a focus on understanding the evolutionary history and diversification of oaks (Quercus).

Before Chicago, I completed an MS in Biology at California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo with Dr. Matt Ritter, and a BA in Political Science (minor in Music and Biology) at the same institution.

kalthaus@uchicago.edu
